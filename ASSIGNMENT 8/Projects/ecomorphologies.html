Ecomorphologies

type: architecture
year: 2017
with: Christine Zhu

Located in Pittsburgh’s Strip District, between McCollough bridge and 21st street by the Allegheny River, this large residential project aims to create environmentally sensitive housing which reacts to both water fl ow and human circulation. Pittburgh mapped as an industrial town. Overlayed Pittsburgh maps of and around the site from 1870, 1890, 1910, 1940, and 2015. Th e riverfront fi rst emerged as a convenient industrial zone, as the river provided as a dump for toxic and waste. “Th e Strip District” is now a part residential and commercial district, close but not connected to the riverfront. Pittsburgh mapped environmentally, signalling number of Combined Sewer Overfl ows. Shows how the area surrounding the site is constantly being polluted, another potential reason the site and the Strip District is currently not connected with the water front.
